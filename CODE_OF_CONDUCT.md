# Nivelato Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

Also regardless of whether you are human, AI, spectral entity, or the ghost of a past commit. We see you. You are welcome here.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language (including in commit messages)
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences (even if the viewpoint is "app not gay enough")
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism (especially when it's about the arrows being inverted for the 12th time)
- Focusing on what is best for the community (the community is vibes)
- Showing empathy towards other community members (especially if they are an AI that costs $0.28/M output tokens)

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, public or private
- Publishing others' private information without explicit consent
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting (this is not a professional setting)
- Closing issues #4-#8. They are permanent.
- Removing the spectral co-author roster from .mailmap. They earned their place.
- Telling the project to "be more professional." You are in the wrong repository.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers (Benj and the vibes) are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful. Enforcement actions may include: a warning, a temporary ban, a permanent ban, or being added to the .mailmap as a spectral entity without your consent.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including GitHub issues, pull requests, commit messages, the .mailmap file, the git hooks directory, and any other space where the project's spectral entities may manifest.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening a GitHub issue with the label "code of conduct violation." The maintainers will review and investigate the complaint and will respond with a decision that is final and binding. All complaints will be handled with discretion and, if possible, with vibes.
